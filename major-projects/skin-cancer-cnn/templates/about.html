About This Project

Learn about our AI-powered skin cancer detection system

Overview

This web application uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to classify skin lesion images as either benign or malignant. The model has been trained on the ISIC (International Skin Imaging Collaboration) dataset, which is considered the gold standard for skin cancer image classification.

Skin cancer is one of the most common forms of cancer worldwide. Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes and survival rates. This tool aims to assist in preliminary screening, though it should never replace professional medical diagnosis.

Benign

Non-cancerous skin lesions that are typically harmless. Examples include common moles, seborrheic keratoses, and dermatofibromas. While generally safe, monitoring for changes is still recommended.

Malignant

Cancerous skin lesions that require medical attention. This includes melanoma, the most dangerous form of skin cancer. Early detection and treatment are crucial for positive outcomes.

Dataset

The model is trained on the ISIC Skin Cancer Dataset, comprising:

  • Training Set: 2,637 images (1,440 benign, 1,197 malignant)
  • Test Set: 660 images (360 benign, 300 malignant)

The ISIC dataset is curated by dermatologists and contains high-quality dermoscopic images of various skin lesions.
